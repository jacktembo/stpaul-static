Brief History Of ST Paul's Mission Hospital

Z.E.N. school started building in 1972 and was opened in 1974 with 25 students. The first Principal was Sr. Lambertha Janssen and her assistant Sr. Marielle Bergeron (White Sister).

St. Paul’s School of Nursing was established in 1974 and Midwifery school in 2010 to provide a two-year and one year training programs for Zambia Enrolled Nurses and Enrolled Midwives respectively. It is a mission institution run by Catholic Church of Mansa Diocese and funded by Co-operating partners and the Government of the Republic of Zambia through the Ministry of Health. The school is situated in Nchelenge District of Luapula Province. It is located within St. Paul’s mission hospital premises to enable student nurses have access to the clinical areas for practice.

The school has greatly expanded following the establishment of the Midwifery school in 2010. In 2017 the school was up graded to registered nursing and first intake started in July 2017 with only 93 students.

The capacity assessment fits perfectly well into the context of world bank project in that the school of nursing though under mission is supported by government to train nurses and achieve the MOH goals of taking the services as close to the family as possible. The objective of the gap assessment and the process that was followed is purely in line with the courses the school offers and the laid down standards in order to trained competent nurses who in future will serve the Zambian people This assessment is valid and very important to the school as well as the students we are training in that

  • Nurses will be in powered with full knowledge in order for them to give quality service to the people
  • It will also help our nation Zambia to reduce on the diseases which can be prevented hence boosting the economy and ending poverty
  • It will help reduce or completely eliminate unnecessary maternal deaths (IMCI)
  • The standards of our institution will be uplifted to meet the global health needs
